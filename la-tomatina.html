12 Days : 05 Hours : 45 Minutes

La Tomatina (Buñol, Spain)

La Tomatina is a famous annual festival held in the town of Buñol, Spain, where participants engage in a massive tomato fight. Taking place on the last Wednesday of August, this unique event attracts thousands of locals and tourists from around the world. The origins of La Tomatina date back to 1945, when a playful scuffle during a parade resulted in people throwing tomatoes at each other. The event was so enjoyable that it became an annual tradition. The festivities begin with a "palo jabón," a challenge where participants attempt to climb a greased pole to retrieve a ham. Once the ham is secured, the tomato fight starts. Trucks bring in over 100,000 kilograms of ripe tomatoes, and for one hour, participants hurl them at each other in a joyful chaos of red splatter. The festival’s rules encourage safety and fun: participants are advised to squish the tomatoes before throwing them to avoid injury and are required to wear protective gear like goggles. After the fight, fire trucks hose down the streets, and the town returns to normal. La Tomatina is celebrated for its fun spirit and unique blend of culture, community, and chaos.
